### Founder Red Flags
- [ ] Serial "ideas person" with no execution
- [ ] Left previous startups quickly (<1 year)
- [ ] No domain expertise in company's space
- [ ] History of founder conflicts
- [ ] Lawsuit or controversy history
- [ ] Excessive self-promotion, little substance
- [ ] No technical co-founder (for tech company)
- [ ] Glassdoor reviews mentioning toxicity

### Founder Green Flags
- [ ] Previous successful exit
- [ ] Deep domain expertise (10+ years)
- [ ] Worked at respected companies
- [ ] Co-founders worked together before
- [ ] Technical founder for technical company
- [ ] Transparent about failures
- [ ] Strong network
- [ ] Mission-driven with authentic story

## Interview Questions for Startups

1. "What's your current runway and next milestones?"
2. "How do you handle co-founder disagreements?"
3. "What's the current ARR/MRR and growth rate?"
4. "What keeps you up at night about this business?"
5. "If the company fails, what will be the reason?"
6. "What's the path to my equity being worth something?"
7. "How is remote work going to work as you scale?"
8. "Tell me about a time the company almost failed."

---

## Deal Breakers

- [ ] <12 months runway, no funding path
- [ ] Founder history of mistreatment
- [ ] No technical leadership
- [ ] "Stealth" with no investor disclosure
- [ ] Equity significantly below market
- [ ] High burn, unclear path to revenue
- [ ] Founder won't discuss financials

---

## Decision Framework

**Accept if**:
- Founder score >= 4 AND
- Investor score >= 3 AND
- Viability score >= 3 AND
- Equity justifies base cut AND
- Can financially survive failure

**Negotiate harder if**:
- Any score is 2-3
- Runway < 18 months
- Base >20% below market

**Pass if**:
- Any critical red flag
- Founder score < 3
- Can't afford failure
- Viability score < 2
